## Scoring Rubric

Each prospect is scored 0-3 on five dimensions (max 15):
- **ICP fit:** high-stakes technical workflow, not generic chatbot
- **Pain evidence:** visible reliability, eval, governance, or human-review problem
- **Urgency:** hiring, shipping, production incidents, active issues, recent posts
- **Reachability:** founder, technical lead, public email, warm intro, active presence
- **Detrix fit:** gates, audit, evaluator feedback, or domain-specific acceptance criteria would clearly help

Priority: total >= 10. Strong lead: total >= 12.

Trust boundary: this matrix uses public web, GitHub/API, official pages, search-engine-indexed snippets, and human-reviewable URLs only. LinkedIn profile pages were not visited or scraped.

## Bucket Summary

### Bucket A: Production-Agent Reliability Teams (70% allocation)

Bucket A contains 21 of 30 prospects. The strongest pattern is regulated or high-value production workflows where agent failures create financial, compliance, legal, healthcare, or enterprise trust risk. These teams already talk in Detrix-native language: evaluation, human audits, drift monitoring, tool-call governance, safety checks, traceability, and promotion control.

Best first-pass targets: Hippocratic AI, Norm Ai, Ensemble Health Partners, Harvey, Hebbia, Gradient Labs, Decagon, Rogo, HumanLayer, and Agentin AI.

### Bucket B: Materials / Science / R&D Teams (30% allocation)

Bucket B contains 9 of 30 prospects. The repeated pain is not generic chatbot reliability; it is decision-grade scientific autonomy where wrong acceptance is costly, uncertainty must be validated, provenance must be replayable, and physical experiments or simulations are the real acceptance surface.

Best first-pass targets: Lila Sciences, Berkeley Lab FORUM-AI, FutureHouse, K-Dense, IndustrialMind, CEDER Group, Yaghi group, Orbital Materials, and CrystaLyse.AI.

- Evaluation has moved from "did the model answer?" to trajectory, tool-call, trace, evaluator, and promotion evidence.
- Regulated production agents need human oversight and auditability, but many public signals still stop at logs or observability rather than domain-scored acceptance.
- Scientific-agent teams care about provenance, uncertainty, reproducibility, simulation/experiment validation, and no-wrong-accept behavior more than generic chatbot quality.

Highest-quality channels:
- Company-level discovery produced the strongest buyer-ready prospects.
- Warm-network search produced the best reachable materials/science targets.
- GitHub issues produced sharp pain language but weaker buyer identity for many authors.

Gaps:
- Few public prospects explicitly say they cannot send sensitive traces to large providers; this should be probed in interviews.
- Materials prospects often expose strong scientific validation pain but less explicit willingness to buy agent-governance software.
- LinkedIn search snippets are useful for discovery but should be manually reviewed before any outreach.
